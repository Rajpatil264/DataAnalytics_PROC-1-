INTRODUCTION:

                Deforestation is the purposeful clearing of forested land. India lost nearly 38.5 thousand hectares (Kha) of tropical forest between 2019 and 2020 making up nearly 14 per cent loss of its tree cover. Meanwhile, the total tropical forest area in India fell by 0.38 percent. The effects of deforestation are not confined to loss of tree cover and unpredictable weather conditions along with adverse monsoon, but are linked to and result in flooding, siltation, soil leaching leading to low and poor agricultural yield.

              So, this project is totally based on the data which shows the total deforested land of India in Hectares.

                          

OBJECTIVES:

           To study the Deforestation in India from 2001 to 2021 and also to study the rate of Deforestation.
                    

 

DESCRIPTION:

The project is based on the State wise study of data of deforested land from 2001 to 2021. Generally, the data shows the total deforested land of State in hectares of five years as follows: {(2001-05), (2006-10), (2011-15), (2016-21)}.

In this project firstly the data of deforested land is collected in the form of Microsoft Excel Spreadsheets.

The Python Programming Language is Used throughout the project. Because of its In-built libraries it is easier to plot the graphs.

Generally, 4 Libraries of Python are used:

1)NumPy: In Python we have lists that serve the purpose of arrays, but they are slow to process. NumPy aims to provide an array object that is up to 50x faster than traditional Python lists.

 

2)Pandas: Pandas makes it simple to do many of the time consuming, repetitive tasks associated with working with data, including Data cleansing, Data fill, Data normalization.

 

3)Seaborn: Seaborn is a library that uses Matplotlib underneath to plot graphs. It will be used to visualize random distributions.

 

4)Matplotlib: Matplotlib is a cross-platform, data visualization and graphical plotting library for Python and its numerical extension NumPy.
